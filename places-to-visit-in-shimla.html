Places to Visit in Shimla

Once regarded as the summer capital of British India, Shimla in Himachal Pradesh, also known as the Queen of Hills, boasts its pleasant climate, colonial architecture from past centuries, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains. From sightseeing to shopping to adventure activities like trekking, it's an all-rounder destination. Places to visit in Shimla range from exploring the trail of historical significance left behind by British colonizers to tasting cultural cuisines that will leave your taste buds tingling and longing for more!

Scandal Point
With romanticism in the air, Scandal Point derives its title from the infamous stir it caused during the British Raj with a case of elopement. The Lover’s point connects lies at the intersection of Mall and Ridge Road. Surrounded by sit-out cafes, restaurants, and shopping opportunities, tourists generally engage in horse riding or just find a cosy spot to relax. It’s one of the most visited destinations to admire the beauty of the hilltops, awe over the dazzling British architecture of buildings, and enjoy the ever-famous ‘pahadi maggi’.

The Ridge
The Ridge is a large open space, famous for being the social and cultural magnet of Shimla. People from all age groups can be spotted, enjoying the scenic view, shopping, and taking photographs. It is home to several government buildings, churches, and museums, as well as a variety of snack shops, restaurants, and cafes. Evenings are the most lively and interesting times, The Ridge comes alive with the colourful artificial waterfalls and in general, the pleasant weather makes it soothing just to hang out. You can also sign up for short-distance horse riding, visit the Lakkar bazaar nearby for souvenirs, stop by Christ’s Church if history interests you, or walk to the Jakhoo Temple.

Summer Hill
Located about 5km from Shimla Ridge, and at an altitude of 2100, above sea level, Summer Hill is regarded for its stunning landscape with a dense forest trail abundant with flora and fauna to explore. Being one of the seven hills that account for Shimla’s mountain ranges, a hike to the hilltop will bless you with unmatched and breathtaking views of the surrounding. It is also one of the many small stops on the Kalka-Shimla Railway. While you’re there, go horseback riding, and visit the Monorville mansion that harboured Mahatma Gandhi, the Summer Hill Gardens, Cemetery, Club, and the Market. Local eateries with flavorful dishes to offer, make Summer Hill one of the compulsory places to visit in Shimla.

Mall Road
Mall Road is one of the busiest streets in Shimla with tourists and locals scattered around, shopping and soaking the essence of the place. It has possibly everything, clothing stores, handcrafted products, jewellery, electronics, clubs, banks and post offices, gift and book shops, and souvenirs for your loved ones. Mall Road is definitely a package deal with extraordinary cafes and restaurants to devour delicious delicacies. You can also extend your trip to nearby touchpoints like scandal point, the town hall, and kali bari temple, a few must-visit places in Shimla.
